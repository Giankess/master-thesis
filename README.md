# master-thesis
The goal of this project is to develop a specialized Natural Language Processing (NLP) pipeline that utilizes transformer-based topic modeling to extract granular, interpretable themes from financial news streams.
Ultimately, this artifact serves as a foundational module for advanced financial econometrics. The resulting pipeline and the generated topic time-series data can be used for several highly impactful applications:
1. Predictive Risk Monitoring and Explainable Credit Risk The topics generated by your pipeline can replace or augment standard quantitative financial ratios in predictive models. By identifying specific risk narratives—such as "regulatory inquiries" or "financing activities"—the pipeline enhances early-warning systems for corporate distress and corporate bond defaults. Furthermore, by applying techniques like SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations) to these topics, analysts can create transparent models that explain exactly which news themes are driving a company's risk assessment.
2. Algorithmic Trading and Dynamic Signal Generation In asset management, the pipeline allows investors to identify the specific semantic themes driving price movements rather than relying on static sentiment. This enables the creation of "market-news attention" mechanisms, where trading algorithms dynamically assign weights to news items based on their thematic relevance to current market conditions, rather than treating all news as equally impactful.
3. Information Filtering and Reducing Overload For financial professionals, the pipeline acts as an automated organizational tool. By dynamically clustering thousands of daily news articles, the system filters out routine reporting and highlights significant, market-moving narratives, drastically reducing information overload without the need for manual intervention.
4. Exogenous Drivers for Self-Exciting Risk Models The time-series data generated by your topics can be integrated into advanced models like multivariate Hawkes processes. Because systemic risk events often exhibit self-exciting clustering behavior, your topics can serve as exogenous variables to help researchers quantify exactly how specific news narratives trigger feedback loops and propagate stress throughout financial markets.
5. Enhanced Volatility and Value-at-Risk (VaR) Modeling The granular topics can be used to refine Value-at-Risk calculations and volatility forecasting. By distinguishing between specific positive and negative news themes, future predictive models can better capture the asymmetric impact that different types of news have on realized market volatility.

First I am aiming to get a good dataset from either finviz or finnhub. I am trying to get the one with the most amount of usable, historical data.
Then I have to clean the data so it is usable. I need the stock, headline/description, datetime and date (without time).

Then I want to compare different topic modelling pipelines (LDA, Top2Vec, BERTopic and BERTopic with FinBERT embeddings) on this dataset. For that I have to implement train/test in all of them.

After I want to (automatically) finetune the best one (probably BERTopic). I want to chronologically split the data into train, val and test. I want to train it on test data and finetune it automatically by trying different combinations of hyperparameters and evaluate them with the val data. As Evaluation methods I want to use for example topic coherence ($C_v$ or $PMI$), topic diversity & redundancy, Intra-topic Similarity vs. Inter-topic Distance, Financial Keyword Density, or just general clustering validation methods.
Lastly I want to then test/evaluate the best model on the test data. I want to also see to what degree the topics explain the stock price movement in a linear regression by checking the r-squared value and significance of topics.
